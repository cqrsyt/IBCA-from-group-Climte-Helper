Privileges and Immunities (Article 5)

The Bank, its assets, premises, and official communications shall be inviolable and immune from every form of legal process, tax, or interference, except insofar as it has expressly waived its immunity. Officials shall enjoy immunity for all acts performed in their official capacity.

Six Pillars of Protection

Legal Processes

Jurisdictional immunity for bank loan decisions.

Assets & Property

Complete protection from financial freezing or seizure.

Premises & Archives

Absolute inviolability against host state intrusion.

Fiscal Status

Tax exemption for all institutional income and property.

Personnel

Staff immunity shielded from personal civil/criminal liability.

Communications

Freedom of unhindered contact with member states.

Accountability Mechanisms

Draft Article 12 provides for appropriate modes of settlement of private-law disputes and establishes that privileges and immunities are granted in the interests of the Bank, not for personal benefit. The Standing Committee has the right and duty to waive immunity where it would impede the course of justice. Supporting mechanisms include an Inspection Panel, Administrative Tribunal, External Audit, and Waiver of Immunity procedures.
