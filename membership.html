Membership

Contributing Members, Recipient Members, and Non-State participation

Two Categories of Membership

Contributing Members

Provide funds to the Bank. Capital contribution is based on GDP. Greater contributions mean more votes under the weighted voting system — a standard of financial institutions.

Recipient Members

Climate-vulnerable countries that benefit from IBCA financing. Priority is given to Least Developed Countries and Small Island Developing States.

Membership Criteria

  • UN Membership — Eligibility is linked to United Nations membership.
  • Capital Donation based on GDP (for Contributing Members) — Reflects contribution capacity and determines weighted voting power.

Differentiated status based on contribution capacity is reflected in weighted voting: greater contributions mean more votes. This is a standard feature of international financial institutions.

Non-State Participation

Technical Advisory Panel

Scientists and experts from non-state institutions provide technical guidance.

Joint Financing Partnership

Commercial banks and investors can partner in co-financing arrangements.

Close Connection with WMO

Strong institutional link with the World Meteorological Organization for climate data and trends that underpin funding decisions.
