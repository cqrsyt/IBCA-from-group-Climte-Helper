Mandate

Preamble and Object & Purposes of the International Bank of Climate Adaptation

Preamble

Concerning

Adverse impacts of climate change, in particular extreme weather events, sea-level rise, and ecosystem degradation, which disproportionately threaten Least Developed Countries and Small Island Developing States.

Recognizing

That the existing international financial institutions and climate funds have inadequately addressed the urgent adaptation needs of the most vulnerable.

Determining

To establish a new international financial institution dedicated exclusively to climate adaptation, including financing resilient infrastructure, scientific research, and ecosystem restoration — founded on sound banking principles, and strictly precluded from diversifying its mandate.

Convincing

That such an institution must empower powerful states to support States which have vulnerable climate through innovative governance, while maintaining robust financial standing.

Reminding

That IBCA is an international organization that all states need to meet their obligations in order to maintain a win-win phenomenon.

Aspiring

To enable effective cooperation with existing specialised international organisations and non-State stakeholders, to maximise synergies and avoid duplication.

Object and Purposes

  1. Exclusive purpose: The Bank’s exclusive purpose shall be to finance climate adaptation in vulnerable regions, prioritising Least Developed Countries and Small Island Developing States, through resilient infrastructure, scientific research, and ecosystem restoration.
  2. No mission creep: The Bank shall not engage in general development lending or any activity outside this purpose, nor shall it diversify its mandate. Any amendment shall require the highest voting threshold under this Agreement.
  3. WMO connection: The Bank will maintain a close connection with the World Meteorological Organization, since the variation trend of the climate in different states is the basis for the funding and other adapting methods during the rapid changing of climate.
  4. Cooperation: The Bank may cooperate with United Nations specialised agencies and existing climate finance institutions, to ensure complementarity, and may partner with non-State stakeholders, including but not limited to civil society, research bodies, and the private sector.
